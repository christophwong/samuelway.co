Profile

I started with Product Design at Chelsea College of Arts & Design which lead to the discovery of The Cordwainers College within London College of Fashion. In 2011, I graduated with a Degree in Footwear Design & Technology and went straight to design for high street brands which outsource nearly all of their manufacturing processes. Knowing that I am better with my hands and my urge for quality product, I went on working with more custom related labels as I joined Dorraj Footwear, a luxury sneaker company in London, and then a bespoke tailoring house, and then, eventually landing an apprenticeship with Master shoemaker Masaru Okuyama where I was able to further hone my shoecrafting skills.

How it's done

Bespoke shoes are always built from scratch; both feet are tape measured and an identical plaster cast is then made. From the cast, a pair of shoe-last is then recreated by copying the curvature and silouette of the foot in order to capture a perfect fit.
